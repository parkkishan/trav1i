More about R.K. Ashrama


The Swami Vivekananda Memorial in Belagavi stands on the historical grounds of the house where Swami Vivekananda spent four days in 1892 during his visit to the region. Established in 2000, the ashrama serves as a center for both spiritual and cultural upliftment, nurturing the youth through various programs like Balak Sangha, Balika Sangha, and Yuvak Sangha, which promote moral values, cultural awareness, and spiritual growth. The ashrama also focuses on community welfare by running an eye clinic and providing essential support to those in need through activities such as distributing ration kits, winter clothes, and offering financial, educational, and medical assistance. In its spiritual realm, the ashrama holds daily worship services, morning and evening bhajans, Ramnam Sankirtan, Japa Yajna, and regularly organized spiritual retreats, meditation classes, and discourses. It also commemorates the birthdays of the Holy Trio, which adds to the sacredness of the place. The Swami Vivekananda Memorial, located in the very house Swami Vivekananda stayed in, was restored and transformed into a memorial in 2019 after the Ramakrishna Mission took full charge in 2007. The mission’s restoration efforts preserved the house's historical significance, making it a tribute to Swami Vivekananda’s teachings. The Memorial offers an exhibition showcasing Swami Vivekananda’s life and Indian culture, along with computer training and classes in Spoken English, Sanskrit, and Yoga. The Memorial is also a hub for youth-focused activities, including lectures, conventions, and annual essay-writing, recitation, and elocution competitions. A temple shrine and Swami Vivekananda monument further enrich the spiritual experience at the memorial. The Swami Vivekananda Memorial and Ashrama serve as an educational, spiritual, and cultural landmark, honoring Swami Vivekananda’s legacy while benefiting the local community through its various program.

Activities

The ashram is dedicated to nurturing moral, cultural, and spiritual values among children and youth through various initiatives. Programs like Balak Sangha, Balika Sangha, and Yuvak Sangha help instill discipline, ethics, and spiritual awareness, shaping young minds with positive values.

Healthcare and Welfare Services :
The ashram actively engages in community welfare, extending support to those in need. It runs an eye clinic to provide essential eye care services. Additionally, it distributes ration kits, winter garments, and financial aid to underprivileged families. Medical and educational assistance is also provided to ensure better healthcare and learning opportunities for those who require support.

Religious and Spiritual Activities :
To promote spiritual well-being, the ashram conducts daily worship, morning and evening bhajans, and Ramnam Sankirtan. Devotees can participate in Japa Yajna, spiritual retreats, meditation sessions, and discourses that foster inner peace and self-growth. The ashram also celebrates the birth anniversaries of the Holy Trio, preserving their teachings and legacy through prayers and special programs. Through these activities, the ashram remains a center of service, spirituality, and community upliftment, enriching lives with knowledge, devotion, and compassion.
